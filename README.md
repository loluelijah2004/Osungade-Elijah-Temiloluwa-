## Who I Am

I'm a biomedical scientist from Nigeria with a B.Sc in Anatomy from Babcock University, transitioning into computational biology with a focused specialization in **Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) genomics**.

My undergraduate thesis on the effects of *Bryophyllum pinnatum*-derived phytochemical compounds on *E. coli*-inoculated Wistar rats gave me my first serious encounter with the AMR problem. I've since committed to attacking it from the genomic and computational angle — building the technical skills to bridge wet-lab biological intuition with data-driven research.

I believe the AMR crisis is one of the most underfunded, underrepresented problems in global health — particularly in low-and-middle-income countries. That's the problem I'm building my career around.

## Focus Area: Antimicrobial Resistance

AMR is projected to kill **10 million people annually by 2050** — disproportionately in Africa and other LMICs. My goal is to contribute to genomics-driven diagnostic tools that are affordable and deployable in resource-limited settings.

Current scientific interest: predicting AMR phenotypes from whole-genome sequencing data using machine learning.
